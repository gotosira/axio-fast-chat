Person with a disability
A person with a disability refers to someone who has a physical, mental, sensory, or intellectual impairment. This person-first language promotes respect and avoids defining someone solely by their condition.

Person living with a disability
A person living with a disability emphasizes that the individual continues to live a full life while managing a physical, sensory, or mental impairment. This phrasing highlights capability and dignity rather than focusing on limitation.

A person who has autism spectrum disorder (ASD), a developmental condition that affects communication, behavior, and social interaction in varying ways. Person-first language reflects respect for individual differences 
and avoids stereotyping based on diagnosis.

the person’s experience and avoids defining them solely by their level of vision.

Person who is blind
A person with this condition would be affected by their ability to see clearly or fully. This condition may require the use of assistive devices or support for navigation and reading. The term uses person-first language to emphasize respect and avoid defining the person by their disability.

Person who is deaf
A person with complete or near-complete hearing loss. Many deaf individuals may use sign language as their primary mode of communication and may identify with Deaf culture. Using person-first language shows respect for the individual and recognizes that deafness is part of their identity, not a limitation.

A person with this condition would be affected by their ability to hear partially or fully. The condition may require the use of hearing aids, sign language, or other communication support. The term uses person-first language to affirm dignity and avoid labeling based on the condition.

Person who uses a wheelchair
A person who needs to use a wheelchair for mobility. This word is considered as inclusive language 
because it emphasizes the ability that a person can do, like using a wheelchair, not focused on a person's limitation or inability.

Person with a mobility impairment
A person with this condition would be affected by their physical movement, balance, or coordination. They may use mobility aids such as wheelchairs, canes, or walkers. Person-first language is used to show respect and avoid reducing the person to their physical limitations.

Person with a speech impairment
A person with this condition would be affected by their ability to communicate clearly with other people. A person with the condition finds it difficult or cannot express thoughts, ideas, or needs effectively. 
The term uses person-first language to show respect and avoid defining the person by their communication 
challenges.

A person who has the Down syndrome, a genetic condition that affects cognitive development and 
physical characteristics. It may cause a flat facial profile, small head and ears, short neck, low muscle tone, loose joints, or shorter limbs. The term uses person-first language that reflects respectful for the individuals, without prioritizing the conditions or reinforcing the stigma.
